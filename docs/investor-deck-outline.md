# Beelapay Investor Deck Outline (10 Slides)

## Deck Goal
Raise pre-seed/seed capital to execute the South Sudan Phase 1 launch and prove the wedge: controlled family finance for diaspora remittances with USSD-first local usability.

## Slide 1 - Cover and Thesis
- Title: Beelapay
- Subtitle: Turning remittances into managed family finance
- One-line thesis: We help diaspora senders fund, control, and monitor household spending in real time.
- Presenter line: Founding team, contact, date

## Slide 2 - Problem (Current State)
- Diaspora senders lack visibility into how money is used after transfer.
- Families rely on fragmented rails and low-connectivity channels.
- Existing options optimize transfer completion, not household-level controls and accountability.
- Close with problem statement: "Money arrives, but trust and control do not."

## Slide 3 - Why Now
- Mobile and wallet behavior is increasing, but feature-phone usage remains high in target market.
- USSD and SMS are still required for mass access.
- Regulatory and partner rails are maturing enough to support a wallet-led model.
- Timing angle: first mover advantage in structured family-finance UX in South Sudan.

## Slide 4 - Product (Phase 1 MVP)
- Master and attached family accounts.
- Multi-currency wallet foundation (launch with limited currency set).
- Spending limits and transaction visibility for master holders.
- Merchant acceptance and one reliable external cash-out/payout rail.
- USSD and SMS for low-connectivity access.
- Visual: one end-to-end user flow (fund -> allocate -> spend -> notify).

## Slide 5 - Product Moat and Differentiation
- Family-linked permission model (not just send-and-forget remittance).
- USSD-first design for local execution at scale.
- Wallet + orchestration + reconciliation layer creates operational moat over time.
- Transaction data foundation for later risk and credit products (with consent-based governance).

## Slide 6 - Market Entry and GTM
- Beachhead: South Sudan corridor with focused diaspora segment.
- Launch shape: one city/merchant cluster, one payout rail, one KYC path.
- Distribution: diaspora community channels, assisted local onboarding, merchant field activation.
- 90-day pilot milestones and adoption funnel.

## Slide 7 - Business Model and Unit Economics Framework
- Revenue streams:
  - FX spread
  - Transaction fees
  - (Later) lending margin
  - (Later) data products
- Core economics to show:
  - CAC per active master wallet
  - Cost per USSD session
  - Gross margin per transfer type
  - Reconciliation break rate and fraud-loss assumptions
- Position this as disciplined expansion from payments to lending, not a bundled day-one promise.

## Slide 8 - Traction Plan and Milestones
- Phase 1 target outcomes (month 6): active wallets, merchants, transaction volume, USSD usage, NPS.
- Build milestones:
  - 0-30 days: scope freeze, compliance baseline, architecture lock
  - 31-60 days: alpha on core ledger and flows
  - 61-90 days: pilot readiness and controlled rollout
- Show KPI dashboard categories: growth, reliability, economics, risk.

## Slide 9 - Risk and Compliance Strategy
- Top risks: licensing delays, rail reliability, FX volatility, fraud/collections, political instability.
- Mitigations:
  - staged rollout by rail
  - strong reconciliation and exception ops
  - conservative limits and controls
  - regulator-first engagement and legal workstream ownership
- Trust section: KYC/AML, auditability, encryption, account security.

## Slide 10 - Funding Ask and Use of Funds
- Ask: amount, instrument, and runway target.
- Use of funds:
  - product and engineering build
  - compliance and licensing execution
  - pilot launch operations and merchant rollout
  - risk/reconciliation controls
- 12-18 month outcomes tied to funding.
- Final line: clear call to action for investor diligence next steps.
